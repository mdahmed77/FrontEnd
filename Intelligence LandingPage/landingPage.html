How has COVID-19 Impacted
the Demand for Medical Cannabis?

Our results show that there was indeed an increased demand for medical cannabis during the pandemic, and its correlation with the COVID-19 cases, as well as other exogenous shocks throughout the year of 2020. Major social events beyond the pandemic ensued in our nation, like the BLM protests and the 2020 elections, which can be seen as spikes in medical cannabis interest in the data graphs. The main reason and result of why people are interested in medical cannabis is for happiness. This nullifies our original hypothesis:

Cannabis sales have indeed increased due to increased demand for medical marijuana to cope with stress, and most patients are seeking to feel “relief” in their health and wellness assessments.

It can be assumed from Veriheal’s patient data set that a large number of people suffered from a depressive episode or had anxiety during the pandemic. We can further extrapolate that America went through times of unrest beyond the pandemic which influenced the amount of signups and bookings through Veriheal. The pandemic, protests, riots, and elections caused major worries and uncertainty for the nation, therefore, medical cannabis is being sought out as a tool for mental health and a means to achieve happiness.

In collaboration with:

Our investigation into the interests and adoption of cannabis by consumers during the COVID-19 pandemic was assessed using patient data provided by Veriheal. Medical cannabis has traditionally been seen and recommended as an alternative medical treatment in relieving physical pain and chronic conditions.

Our original hypothesis was constructed with this assumption in mind, we believed that the effect of interest would be “relief”. The relationship between interest in cannabis which is represented by the number of sign-ups in addition to bookings which is indicative of adoption, overall increased throughout the COVID-19 pandemic.

However, contrary to our beliefs it can be inferred that patients we’re looking to cannabis for alleviation of mental stresses, “Happy/Bliss” was the most desired effect while “relief” was the second by a much lesser degree.

While our initial hypothesis was disproved by the desire for phycological relief as opposed to physical offers room for exploration into what cannabis has represented during COVID and understanding its place post-COVID.
